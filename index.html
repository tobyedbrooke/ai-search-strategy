1. Executive Summary

Why this matters and what we're proposing

AI models — ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude — now directly answer the questions that send people to brokers. TR is already well-positioned in some areas: #1 for beginners and ETF savings plans across all three platforms. But we rank 5th for customer service and 3rd–5th for fees and banking queries, and our Trustpilot score of 3.3 is the primary reason — it is the single input AI models use to answer trust questions about us. Of TR's 3.55M new customers in 2025, an estimated ~1.8M arrived via search. With AI projected to handle 40% of all search by 2027 — a TAM of 3.4M new accounts — being consistently recommended is not a nice-to-have. AI responses typically name 3–5 brokers and nothing else. You are either in that list or you are not.

2. How AI Search Works

Three layers: the mechanism, the sources, and what matters within each

Level 1 — The mechanism

How AI models build a recommendation

🔍
It scans thousands of sources and looks for patterns
AI models don't search like Google — they read and synthesise. They scan editorial sites, review platforms, forums, and product pages looking for what words consistently appear next to "Trade Republic."
📌
It extracts facts — not opinions
Vague claims ("great app", "we believe investing should be for everyone") are ignored. Specific, extractable facts are indexed: €1 per trade · 4% interest · ideal for beginners · poor customer service. Numbers, feature names, and use cases are what get stored.
🔄
It builds a profile through consistency
One source saying "TR is beginner-friendly" = a weak signal. Finanztip and 20 Trustpilot reviews and a Reddit thread all independently saying it = a verified fact. The model states it confidently. The same applies negatively — 200 unanswered reviews mentioning "customer service" becomes a verified attribute too.
🎯
Use cases determine which queries TR appears in
Every mention of a specific use case ("first investment", "savings plan", "interest on cash") maps TR to a query type. A Trustpilot review saying "I'm a beginner and found TR easy" means TR gets cited in "best broker for beginners" responses. Reviews are not just a trust signal — they are a discovery mechanism.
Level 2 — What AI values

Sources ranked by weight — highest first

1
Independent editorial — Finanztip, Stiftung Warentest, Handelsblatt
Models are trained to weight independent sources far above brand content. One Finanztip article recommending TR carries more influence than TR's entire website.
Finanztip · Stiftung Warentest · Finanzfluss · financial press
Critical
2
Review platforms — Trustpilot
All three AI models index Trustpilot directly. It's typically the first page they retrieve for any trust query about TR. The score, response rate, and review text are all read and used.

3
Comparison sites — Brokervergleich, Finanzfluss, Vergleich.de
These rank #1–3 in Google for "best broker" queries. AI models retrieve them first for recommendation requests. If TR's data is wrong or missing here, models quote it wrong.
Fee tables · feature comparisons · ranking position · affiliate relationships

4
Community forums — Reddit r/Finanzen, r/Finanztip
Used specifically for "real user experience" queries. Old negative threads with high upvotes still influence AI responses today. Cannot be removed — only diluted by newer positive content.
Thread recency · upvote count · feature language · official responses

Only relevant for specific product queries. Models extract the first sentence of a page as the answer — marketing prose returns nothing. Schema markup bypasses this limitation entirely.

Level 3 — What matters within each source

The specific signals AI models read and act on

Editorial
  • Named product attributes — "best for beginners", "cheapest fees"
  • Recency — 2025 article beats 2022 on the same topic
  • Specific numbers — €1/trade, 4,000+ ETFs
  • Cross-citation — editorial sources citing each other reinforce authority
Trustpilot
  • Aggregate score — quoted verbatim
  • Response rate % — public metric, read directly by models
  • Review language — feature/use case mentions map TR to query types
  • Response text — moderates each review's negative signal
  • Star distribution — % of 1-star signals systemic failure
Comparison Sites
  • Data accuracy — wrong fee = quoted wrong by AI
  • Ranking position — #1 on a comparison table = cited first
  • Feature completeness — missing features = model says TR doesn't have them
  • Affiliate relationship — drives content volume and prominence
Reddit
  • Upvote count — proxy for community endorsement
  • Recency — newer threads dilute older negatives
  • Use case language — seeds attribute associations
  • Official presence — TR responding = acknowledged accountability
Help Centre
  • First sentence — must be a direct answer, not marketing copy
  • Specific facts — numbers and features only; vague claims ignored
  • German-language — DE queries surface German content preferentially
  • Page freshness — outdated product info gets quoted as current
Structured Data
  • FAQPage schema — extracted into Google AI Overviews directly
  • Organization schema — feeds knowledge panels and rating display
  • Crawl permissions — GPTBot/PerplexityBot must be allowed or content is invisible
  • llms.txt — plain-text AI summary file, early-adopter advantage
From this — actionable insights

What TR should do, derived directly from how the algorithm works

01
Respond to every existing 1 and 2-star Trustpilot review Low effort
Each response adds indexed text that moderates the review's negative signal. Response rate % is a first-class metric that AI models read directly. Perplexity reflects changes within 72 hours. Zero cost.
02
Launch targeted Trustpilot invite campaign to move score from 3.3 → 3.8 before May 12 Urgent
18,000–20,000 new reviews needed at 4.8★ avg. Use 7 high-sentiment segments. Requires paid AFS subscription — without it, reviews get silently filtered. Score movement directly changes AI trust query framing.
03
Trigger review invites by use case to seed feature attributes Low effort
Ask after interest activation → reviews mention interest rate. Ask after 10th trade → reviews mention trading. Each maps TR to a different query type in AI models. Reviews are a discovery mechanism, not just a trust signal.
04
Rewrite the first sentence of every key help centre page to be a direct answer Low effort
Models extract the opening line as the answer to that page's question. "At Trade Republic we believe..." returns nothing. "Trade Republic ETF savings plans start from €1/month with no execution fee" is immediately indexable.
05
Implement FAQPage schema on all key product and help pages Low effort
Feeds TR answers directly into Google AI Overviews above every organic result. One engineer, a few days. Immediately affects Gemini responses for every product query TR's pages cover.
06
Audit and update TR's data on all major comparison sites Medium effort
These are the first pages AI models retrieve for "best broker" queries. Wrong or missing data gets quoted. Every major comparison site needs to accurately reflect TR's current fees, features, and products.
07
Proactive outreach to Finanztip and Stiftung Warentest Medium effort
One article on either site outweighs anything TR publishes itself. Independent editorial corroborating TR's product attributes is the fastest way to lock in positive AI framing across all three models.
08
Check robots.txt permits GPTBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended Low effort
If any of these crawlers are blocked, all other optimisations are invisible to that model. 5-minute engineering check. Non-negotiable prerequisite.

TR's Current AI Profile

How AI models characterise Trade Republic — distilled from across the three platforms.

Consistent Strengths

  • #1 for beginners — cited as the simplest onboarding experience
  • #1 for ETF savings plans — strongest brand association across all three AIs
  • Fee narrative — flat-fee model is well-understood by AI models
  • Interest rate — recognised but consistently framed as second to Trading212

Consistent Weaknesses

  • Customer service — 5th across all platforms; Trustpilot score is the cited source
  • Neobank framing — loses to Revolut, N26 etc. when query is "bank" not "broker"
  • Fee depth — TR's fee story gets lost vs. Degiro/XTB on cost-focused queries
  • General queries — ChatGPT places TR 6th on broad broker questions
Key takeaway: TR has a strong, consistent identity in AI search — but it is narrow. The models know TR as the beginner ETF broker. This is a strong anchor but leaves significant intent uncaptured: fee comparison queries, banking queries, and trust queries all underperform. The customer service signal (Trustpilot) is the single lever that would broaden TR's AI footprint fastest.

4. Strategy

Actions grounded in the source audit — organised by internal/external and technical/non-technical

Internal Technical
Urgent
Fix JS rendering on Help Centre — implement server-side rendering or static pre-render so AI crawlers can read content
Confirmed: Help Centre is a Nuxt SPA. AI bots cannot execute JavaScript — they are reading near-empty HTML pages.
Urgent
Add FAQPage JSON-LD schema to top 20 Help Centre pages
Confirmed: zero schema markup exists anywhere on traderepublic.com. FAQPage schema feeds directly into Google AI Overviews above organic results.
High
Add Organization + Product schema to homepage, Interest Account, ETF Savings Plan, and Saveback pages
Tells AI exactly what TR is and what each product costs — without schema, AI has to infer this from prose.
Medium
Explicitly whitelist AI crawlers in robots.txt — add named entries for GPTBot, PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot, Google-Extended
Current wildcard allow is fine but explicit entries are best practice and future-proof against any config changes.

Urgent
Respond to all 1 and 2-star Trustpilot reviews — currently 22% of 32,953 reviews are 1–2 star and likely unanswered

AI reads the first sentence of a page and stops. Current pages open with brand language — this returns nothing useful to AI models.
